Popular Malaysian Cuisines

Nasi Lemak

A fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk, served with sambal, fried crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts, and boiled eggs.

Laksa

A spicy noodle soup with a mix of Chinese and Malay flavors, often containing coconut milk, curry, and various herbs.

Roti Canai

A type of Indian-influenced flatbread that's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, typically served with dhal or curry.

Char Kway Teow

A popular street food, this stir-fried flat noodle dish is cooked with prawns, eggs, Chinese sausages, bean sprouts, and chives, all in a savory and slightly sweet soy sauce.

Satay

Skewered and grilled meat, usually chicken or beef, served with a peanut dipping sauce, rice cakes, and a simple salad of cucumbers and onions.

Hainanese Chicken Rice

Originating from Chinese cuisine, it consists of poached chicken and seasoned rice, served with chili sauce and usually with cucumber garnishes.

Rendang

A slow-cooked dry curry, typically made with beef, though chicken and mutton are also used. It's rich with spices and coconut milk, and cooked until the meat is tender and flavorful.

Penang Assam Laksa

A tangy and spicy noodle soup with a fish-based broth, typically made with mackerel, and flavored with tamarind, lemongrass, and torch ginger flower.

Bak Kut Teh

A pork rib soup cooked with a complex broth of herbs and spices, including star anise, cinnamon, and cloves. It's often served with rice or youtiao (fried dough sticks).
